Prompt Best Practices

üéØ Be Specific

  • Define the role you want AI to assume
  • Specify your audience and context
  • Include relevant constraints (word count, format, tone)
  • Provide examples of desired output

üìã Structure Your Prompts

  • Use clear sections: Role, Task, Context, Requirements
  • Break complex requests into numbered steps
  • Specify output format explicitly
  • Include "do" and "don't" instructions

üîÑ Iterate and Refine

  • Start with a draft, then improve based on output
  • Ask follow-up questions to refine results
  • Save effective prompts for reuse
  • Test prompts across different AI tools

‚öñÔ∏è Use AI Responsibly

  • Always review and verify AI output
  • Maintain your authentic voice and judgment
  • Be transparent about AI use when appropriate
  • Protect student privacy in prompts

üéì Higher Ed Considerations

  • Align AI use with institutional policies
  • Model responsible AI use for students
  • Design AI-resistant assessments when needed
  • Use AI to enhance, not replace, your expertise

  • Perplexity: Research with citations
  • NotebookLM: Document-grounded analysis
  • ChatGPT: Custom GPTs, creative tasks
  • Claude: Long-form writing, analysis
  • Copilot: Microsoft 365 integration
  • Gemini: Google Workspace, multimodal
